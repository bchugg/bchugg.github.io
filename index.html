Hello! I have big ears and I'm addicted to podcasts. I was much cuter as a child.

I'm currently an M.Sc. student at the Mathematical Institute at Oxford. My research interests lie somewhere between graph theory, algorithm design, and guiness—although I'm quickly falling in love with economics. I'm excited to be joining Stanford Law School next year as an empirical research fellow.

I'm sympathetic to Effective Altruism and sat on the committee of EA Oxford. Some of my work is here.

Bio

I spent the previous summer at RIKEN AIP in Tokyo, where I was lucky to work with Dr. Maehara as part of the Discrete Optimization Unit. I spent most of my time confused about submodular stochastic optimization problems.

Prior to that, I spent several wonderful years at the University of British Columbia studying mathematics and computer science. Here my confusion was distributed in approximately equal parts between stochastic chemical reaction networks and subtopics in computational geometry. I was immensely fortunate to be supervised by Will Evans and Anne Condon, who both abated my confusion on many occasions. I wrote my undergraduate thesis on computing in dynamic environments under uncertainty.

During my undergraduate I founded Code the Change UBC, which is now its own foundation (and is much better run).
