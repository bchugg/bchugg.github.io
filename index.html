I have big ears and I'm addicted to podcasts.

I'm currently a research fellow at Stanford Law School's RegLab where I design and evaluate algorithms for environmental regulation and tax enforcement.

I co-host the Increments podcast, where Vaden Masrani and I confuse ourselves and others about politics, philosophy, and science.

I'm on the Giving What We Can events team, and I'm generally sympathetic to Effective Altruism.

An erratic bio

Prior to Stanford, I was a student at the Mathematical Institute at Oxford, where I studied the intersection of spectral graph theory and geometry.

In 2018, I spent some time at RIKEN AIP in Tokyo, where I was lucky to work with Dr. Maehara as part of the Discrete Optimization Unit.

My undergraduate was spent at the University of British Columbia studying mathematics and computer science, where I was immensely fortunate to be supervised by Will Evans and Anne Condon.
