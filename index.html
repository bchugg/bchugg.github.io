Hi, I'm Ben. I'm an undergraduate student at the University of British Columbia studying mathematics and computer science. I'm exceptionally fortunate to be supervised by Will Evans for my thesis research, and by Anne Condon for my research in Stochastic Chemical Reaction Network Theory.

I'm slightly too ambitious for my own good. Consequently, my research interests span a wide variety of topics. I'm interested in computational geometry, randomized algorithms, chemical reaction networks and the closely related model of population protocols, submodular functions and their applications, and algorithms in general.

More broadly, I'm interested in cross-disciplinary applications of computer science to health care, genomics and the non-profit sector. I'm the founder of Code the Change UBC, an organization of students providing free software for non-profits who cannot afford it otherwise. We are a derivative of Code the Change at Stanford. I'm also the co-founder of the Friends of Grace Orphan Care Society, a non-profit helping a small, locally run Malawian Orphan Care achieve sustainability.
