Hello! I have big ears and I'm addicted to podcasts. I was much cuter as a child.

I'm currently an M.Sc. student at the Mathematical Institute at Oxford. My research interests lie somewhere between stochastic networks, biological computation and guiness, although I'm quickly falling in love with economics.

I spent the previous summer at RIKEN AIP in Tokyo, where I was lucky to work with Dr. Maehara as part of the Discrete Optimization Unit. Here I spent most of my time confused about submodular stochastic optimization problems. Prior to that, I spent several wonderful years at the University of British Columbia studying mathematics and computer science. While there, my confusion was distributed in approximately equal parts between stochastic chemical reaction networks and subtopics in computational geometry. I was immensely fortunate to be supervised by Will Evans and Anne Condon, who both abated my confusion on many occasions. I wrote my undergraduate thesis on computing in dynamic environments under uncertainty.
