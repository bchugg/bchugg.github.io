Ben Chugg

Hello! I'm an undergraduate student at the University of British Columbia studying mathematics and computer science. I'm exceptionally fortunate to be supervised by Will Evans for my thesis research, and by Anne Condon for my research in Stochastic Chemical Reaction Network Theory.

My main research interests fall into the broad category of theoretical computer science. I've done research in the areas of computational geometry, graph theory and chemical reaction networks. I'm further intrigued by the areas of randomized algorithms, population protocols, and submodular functions.

More broadly, I'm interested in cross-disciplinary applications of computer science to health care, genomics and the non-profit sector. I'm the founder of Code the Change UBC, an organization of students providing free software for non-profits who cannot afford it otherwise. We are a derivative of Code the Change at Stanford. I'm also the co-founder of the Friends of Grace Orphan Care Society, a non-profit helping a small, locally run Malawian Orphan Care achieve financial sustainability.
