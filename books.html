Nine Favourites

Animal Liberation
Peter Singer
A forceful ethical argument which helped initiate the animal welfare movement. Singer points out that the logic enabling animal cruelty can justify any kind of bigotry.
A Conflict of Visions
Thomas Sowell
A breathtaking classification of the different visions of human nature and how they manifest in political and moral disagreement.
Conjectures and Refutations
Karl Popper
A simple thesis — that we learn by trial and error — with earthshaking consequences.
Crime and Punishment
Fyodor Dostoevsky
A journey into a fascinating psychology.
The Drunkard's Walk
Leonard Mlodinow
How easy it is to be fooled by randomness.
Expert Political Judgement
Philip Tetlock
How accurate are political pundits? Are they incentivized to be accurate? Which sorts of cognitive styles lend themselves to making better predictions?
Gödel, Escher, Bach
Douglas Hofstadter
A fabulous fugue on emergence, self-reference, mathematics, tortoises and gramophones.
The Royal Game
Stefan Zweig
Ever tried to play chess against yourself?
Strangers Drowning
Larissa MacFarquhar
An exploration of what it looks like to truly live by your ethical principles.
